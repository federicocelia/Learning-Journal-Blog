Hi, I’m Federico Celia — a Frontend Developer in Continuous Progress

I’m a self‑driven web developer with a strong interest in building accessible, well‑structured interfaces. My journey into web development has been shaped by hands‑on learning, consistent practice, and a commitment to understanding how the web truly works beyond surface‑level visuals.

How I started

My interest in web development grew alongside my background in computer science studies, where I was introduced to the fundamentals of logic, problem‑solving, and structured thinking. What began as curiosity quickly evolved into a practical pursuit: learning how browsers interpret HTML, how CSS controls layout and presentation, and how JavaScript brings interactivity to the user experience. Through this process, I developed a strong appreciation for clean markup, semantic structure, and the importance of accessibility from the very beginning.

Learning, building, and staying committed

I’ve continued to deepen my skills through structured learning paths, personal projects, and immersive bootcamp experiences, where code reviews and real‑world constraints helped refine my approach. I stay committed to continuous development by actively building, revisiting fundamentals, and treating every project as an opportunity to improve. For me, learning web development isn’t a one‑time goal—it’s an ongoing process of growth, curiosity, and refinement.
